# DongHo_MicesWedding

## Basic Information

- Vietnamese title: Tranh Đông Hồ - Đám cưới chuột
- English title: Dong Ho Folk Painting - The Mice's Wedding
- Category: Vietnamese Traditional Folk Art
- Medium: Woodblock print on Dzo (Điệp) paper
- Origin: Dong Ho Village, Bac Ninh, Vietnam

## Short Introduction

"The Mice's Wedding" (Đám cưới chuột) is one of the most famous and iconic pieces of Dong Ho folk painting. The artwork depicts a lively and humorous scene of a mice procession attempting to celebrate a wedding. However, to ensure their safety, they must first offer bribes—a bird and a fish—to a large, intimidating cat. It is a masterpiece of folk satire that combines vibrant visuals with profound social commentary.

## Concept

The artwork explores the complex social dynamics of ancient Vietnamese feudal society. It is not merely a cute animal story; it is a sharp political satire. The giant cat represents the greedy and oppressive ruling class. The mice represent the ordinary peasants—powerless but clever and resilient. The act of offering gifts to the cat symbolizes the bribes the poor had to pay to live in peace. Ultimately, it reflects the Vietnamese philosophy of flexibility and the strong desire for a peaceful, harmonious life despite adversity.

## Visual Features

- The layout is divided into two tiers: the top tier shows the bribery, while the bottom tier shows the joyful wedding procession.
- The Cat is drawn disproportionately large, emphasizing its power and dominance over the smaller mice.
- The color palette uses natural dyes (red from gravel, yellow from pagoda tree flowers, green from indigo leaves, black from bamboo ash) printed on sparkling seashell (Điệp) paper.
- The lines are bold, simple, and highly expressive, characteristic of traditional woodblock printing techniques.

## Creative Process

Dong Ho paintings are created using traditional woodblock printing techniques without modern machinery. Each color requires a separate carved wooden block. The artisan prints the colors one by one, letting the paper dry in between, and finally stamps the black outline block to bring the whole image to life. This piece rewards careful observation, as each mouse's expression and posture reveals a different emotion—from fear and hesitation to joy and pride.

## Design Interpretation

"The Mice's Wedding" is a visual metaphor for survival and social hierarchy. The AR interaction brings this static woodblock print to life by allowing viewers to decode its layers. By interacting with the separated floating items (the cat, the gifts, the bride, and the groom), users can actively uncover the hidden satirical narrative rather than just seeing a simple animal drawing. The AR experience turns a flat illustration into a spatial story about human resilience.
